What is Canine Distemper?

Canine distemper is a highly contagious viral disease that affects dogs, caused by the canine distemper virus (CDV). It primarily affects the respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems and can be fatal if left untreated.

Symptoms of Canine Distemper in Dogs:

  • Fever and loss of appetite
  • Runny nose and eye discharge
  • Coughing and difficulty breathing
  • Vomiting and diarrhea
  • Seizures and muscle twitching (in severe cases)

Causes:

  • Infection with the canine distemper virus (CDV)
  • Exposure to infected animals or contaminated environments
  • Unvaccinated dogs are at the highest risk
