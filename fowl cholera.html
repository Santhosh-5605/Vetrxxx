Fowl Cholera in Hens

What is Fowl Cholera?

Fowl cholera is a highly contagious bacterial infection caused by *Pasteurella multocida*. It affects chickens and other poultry, causing sudden death, lethargy, and a variety of severe symptoms. It can spread rapidly in a flock, and if not treated promptly, it can lead to significant losses.

Symptoms of Fowl Cholera in Hens:

  • Sudden death (often the first sign)
  • Lethargy and weakness
  • Swollen joints and legs
  • Nasal discharge and diarrhea
  • Pale comb and wattles

Causes:

  • Bacterial infection from *Pasteurella multocida*
  • Contact with infected animals, birds, or contaminated environments
  • Poor sanitation and overcrowding in poultry housing

Treatment and Prevention:

Treatment typically involves antibiotics to control the bacterial infection. Early diagnosis and swift action are critical. Preventative measures include vaccination, good hygiene practices, proper sanitation of coops, and avoiding overcrowding to reduce the risk of infection.
