Intestinal Parasites

What are Intestinal Parasites?

Intestinal parasites are a common health issue in hens. These parasites, including roundworms, tapeworms, and coccidia, live in the digestive system and cause a range of symptoms such as poor egg production, weight loss, and diarrhea.

Symptoms of Fleas and Ticks in Hens:

  • Weight loss or stunted growth
  • Diarrhea or abnormal droppings
  • Pale comb and wattles
  • Reduced egg production
  • Fluffy or unkempt feathers

Causes:

  • Ingestion of contaminated food or water
  • Contact with infected feces or other hens
  • Dirty or poorly maintained living environments

Treatment and Prevention:

Treatment involves using dewormers or anti-parasitic medications that are specifically designed for poultry. It's also essential to keep the living environment clean and dry to prevent re-infestation. Regularly inspecting hens for signs of infection and maintaining good hygiene practices can help prevent intestinal parasite infestations.
