Feline Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) in Cats

What is Feline Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)?

A Feline Urinary Tract Infection (UTI) is a bacterial infection affecting the urinary system of a cat. UTIs can cause discomfort and pain, and if left untreated, can lead to more severe health issues like kidney infections or bladder stones.

Symptoms of Feline UTI in Cats:

  • Frequent urination or attempts to urinate
  • Straining or crying while urinating
  • Blood in the urine
  • Frequent licking of the genital area
  • Foul-smelling urine
  • Loss of appetite or lethargy

Causes:

  • Bacterial infections (most common cause)
  • Bladder stones or crystals
  • Underlying diseases such as diabetes or kidney disease
  • Stress or changes in the environment (can trigger urinary issues)

Treatment and Prevention:

Treatment for a UTI typically involves antibiotics to eliminate the infection. If there are underlying causes, such as bladder stones, additional treatment may be necessary. Increasing water intake, feeding a balanced diet, and keeping your cat stress-free are important steps in preventing UTIs. In severe cases, surgery may be required to remove bladder stones or correct other issues.
