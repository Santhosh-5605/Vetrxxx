Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD) in Cows

What is Foot and Mouth Disease?

Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD) is a highly contagious viral disease affecting cloven-hoofed animals, including cattle. It causes fever, blister-like sores in the mouth and hooves, and can lead to severe economic losses in the livestock industry.

Symptoms of FMD in Cows:

  • Fever and loss of appetite
  • Blisters and sores in the mouth, tongue, and hooves
  • Excessive drooling and difficulty eating
  • Lameness and reluctance to move
  • Reduced milk production

Causes:

  • Direct contact with infected animals
  • Contaminated feed, water, or farm equipment
  • Spread through air, saliva, milk, and feces

Treatment and Prevention:

There is no specific treatment for FMD, but supportive care can help affected animals recover. Prevention strategies include vaccination, strict biosecurity measures, quarantining new animals, and controlling animal movement to prevent the spread of infection.
