Pneumonia in Goats

What is Pneumonia?

Pneumonia is a serious respiratory infection in goats caused by bacteria, viruses, or environmental stressors. It affects the lungs, leading to breathing difficulties and potential fatality if left untreated.

Symptoms of Pneumonia in Goats:

  • Labored or rapid breathing
  • Frequent coughing or nasal discharge
  • Fever and lethargy
  • Loss of appetite and weight loss
  • Isolation from the herd

Causes:

  • Bacterial or viral infections (e.g., Pasteurella, Mycoplasma)
  • Sudden temperature changes or cold, damp conditions
  • Poor ventilation in barns or shelters
  • Weakened immune system due to stress or malnutrition

Treatment and Prevention:

Treatment typically involves antibiotics prescribed by a veterinarian, along with supportive care such as keeping the goat warm and well-hydrated. Prevention includes ensuring proper ventilation, minimizing stress, providing a balanced diet, and vaccinating against common respiratory infections.
