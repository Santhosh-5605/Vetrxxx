Feline Leukemia in Cats

What is Feline Leukemia?

Feline Leukemia Virus (FeLV) is a viral infection that affects cats and can lead to severe health problems, including immune system suppression, anemia, and certain types of cancer. It is a major cause of death in cats, particularly those that are not vaccinated against the disease.

Symptoms of Feline Leukemia in Cats:

  • Loss of appetite and weight loss
  • Persistent fever
  • Anemia (pale gums and lethargy)
  • Swollen lymph nodes
  • Chronic infections or recurring illnesses
  • Behavioral changes such as increased aggression or hiding

Causes:

  • Contact with infected cats, especially through saliva, urine, and feces
  • Close contact with an infected cat during grooming, fighting, or sharing food bowls
  • Mother-to-kitten transmission during pregnancy or birth

Treatment and Prevention:

There is no cure for Feline Leukemia, but cats diagnosed with FeLV can live longer with supportive care. This may include managing secondary infections, providing immune support, and offering a nutritious diet. Prevention is best achieved through vaccination, avoiding contact with infected cats, and keeping infected cats indoors to prevent the spread of the virus.
