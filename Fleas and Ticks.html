Fleas and Ticks in Hens

What are Fleas and Ticks?

Fleas and ticks are external parasites that can infest hens, causing discomfort, irritation, and sometimes serious health problems. Fleas are small, wingless insects that feed on the blood of animals, while ticks are arachnids that also feed on blood and can transmit diseases.

Symptoms of Fleas and Ticks in Hens:

  • Constant itching and scratching
  • Visible fleas or ticks on the skin and feathers
  • Restlessness and reduced egg production
  • Pale comb and wattles (in severe infestations)

Causes:

  • Exposure to other infested animals or birds
  • Poorly maintained chicken coops with high humidity
  • Contact with wild birds or rodents

Treatment and Prevention:

Treatment involves removing the fleas and ticks from the hen using insecticidal shampoos or powders specifically designed for poultry. It's also essential to treat the environment by cleaning the coop thoroughly and using insecticides to eliminate any parasites present. Preventative measures include maintaining a clean and dry environment, using natural deterrents like diatomaceous earth, and regularly inspecting hens for pests.
