Canine Kennel Cough in Dogs

What is Canine Kennel Cough?

Canine Kennel Cough, also known as infectious tracheobronchitis, is a highly contagious respiratory disease in dogs. It is caused by a combination of viruses and bacteria, including the canine parainfluenza virus, canine adenovirus, and Bordetella bronchiseptica. The disease is characterized by a dry, hacking cough, and it is most commonly spread in environments where dogs are in close contact, such as kennels or dog parks.

Symptoms of Canine Kennel Cough in Dogs:

  • Persistent dry, hacking cough
  • Gagging or retching after coughing
  • Watery eyes and nasal discharge
  • Lethargy and loss of appetite (in some cases)
  • Low-grade fever (in some cases)

Causes:

  • Exposure to infected dogs, often in crowded environments like kennels, dog parks, or shelters
  • Weak immune systems, especially in puppies and older dogs
  • Infection with a combination of bacteria and viruses, particularly Bordetella bronchiseptica

Treatment and Prevention:

Treatment for kennel cough typically involves antibiotics if a bacterial infection is present, along with medications to relieve symptoms like coughing. Most cases of kennel cough are mild and resolve on their own, but severe cases may require additional medical care. Prevention includes vaccination against Bordetella bronchiseptica and avoiding high-risk areas, such as crowded kennels or dog parks.
